Midnight Limestone

Midnight Limestone is an extremely dense limestone quarried in Central China. It is used extensively in local villages as veneer, stair block, and paving. This material is also used to make carved objects. A dolomitic limestone, Midnight Limestone almost appears to be a hard marble with its minimal white tracery veining. Black limestones are extremely rare since the organic materials that created them are typically bleached out by strong sunlight. However, somewhere in the deep swamp or bog of its ancient past, this material was heated and compressed over the millennia to become stone. Its unique color ranges from dark charcoal to deep black with luminescent minerals that glimmer against the midnight surface. Due to the consistent structure of the material, Midnight Limestone will accept point stalk texture as fine as four lines per inch, which creates an unmatched visual rhythm when used as paving. Midnight Limestone is best mortar set on a slab to avoid edge chipping.
