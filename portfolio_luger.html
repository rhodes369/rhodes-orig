Luger Residence

Project Principals

Architecture: Tom Kuniholm Architects

Interior Design: Pamela Pearce Design

Built in the grand style of an English country Manor House, this residence sits on a premier acreage in Greenwich, Connecticut. One of the largest homes in the US built in modern times, the plan of the residence cuts in and out to create private courtyards, gardens and myriad visual surprises. With 286 separate elevations on the residence, the steady repeat of the stone vernacular successfully belies the complexity of the architectural form.

More than 200 containers of both antique and hand-crafted stone material (3,400 pallets) form the stone veneer and hardscape. The drawing set for the stone detailing exceeded 350 full-sized sheets. The stone design and detailing were entirely the product of Rhodes Architectural Stone.

The project features Antique Yangtze Limestone, recycled from the rising tide of the Three Gorges Dam Project in China. The dimensional stone pieces were hand-fabricated and hand-textured from freshly quarried New Yangtze Limestone; the modern source quarry for the antique recycled material. When this material is processed using the RAS- 12th century hand-finish, it blends perfectly with the antique material, creating a seamless visual pallet.
