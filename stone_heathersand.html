Stone

Heather Sandstone

Installed Photos

Description

Quarried in rural north China, Heather Sandstone evinces gentle waves of warm and cool hues reminiscent of the wind rippling across a field of heather. The tight grain of Heather Sandstone handles detail exceedingly well, making it an excellent choice for patterning and carving detail. We recommend it for application as 2.5-3"pavements in lychee, fine and coarse adze, or pineapple. The material is often used for interior pavements in the honed finish, which illuminates the movement of this very subtle material. When carved for sinks or one-piece tubs from a single block, the stone revels exceptionally beautiful rings as the discolored layers are carved through. Additionally, when the material is used for stone walls in a split-face texture, it takes on the look of architectural concrete. Heather Sandstone's distinctive color comes from a consistent blend of light purple, coral and rose-colored sediment. Pavers in this material can be sand set or mortar-set on a slab.

Heather Sandstone is a dense, fine-grained sandstone. The material handles detail exceedingly well, making it an excellent choice for patterning and carving. The understated tones of plum and earth produce the same gentle waves of color found in its namesake: blooming fields of heather. Heather Sandstone's distinctive and sophisticated palette comes from a consistent blend of light purple, coral, and rose-colored sediments. Tooling marks from our fine hand-finishes lighten the stone and create a cool lavender color to contrast the warmer earth tones of the unmarked surface. Heather Sandstone is ideal for interior pavements in a honed finish that illuminates the color movement of this very subtle material. When carved into sinks or one-piece tubs from a single block, the stone reveals exceptionally beautiful rings where the colored layers are exposed as veins. Heather Sandstone may be installed in any of the standard setting techniques: mortar or thinset on slab or by sand-setting.

Heather Sandstone is only available if we see "serious evidence of a very big project".
