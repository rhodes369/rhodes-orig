Stone

New Abbey Limestone

Installed Photos

Description

Evocative of plainsong chants resonating beneath gothic arches and time-worn patinas inside monastic walls, New Abbey Limestone echoes the earliest building traditions in Europe. This largely monochromatic stone, while neutral in color, presents a slightly mottled surface with restrained movement and hints of deep complexity. The dance of movement and form assures its identity as stone; unlike many of its rivals, New Abbey Limestone will never be mistaken for precast concrete. Similar to its cousin Pearl Limestone, New Abbey Limestone offers immediate and elegant translucence, a vision of depth and warmth. The high density of New Abbey Limestone resists natural degradation over time, and bestows a surface that becomes smoother year by year. It gains a rich glow where subjected to heavy foot traffic or other kinds of natural wear, producing a dazzling and rich result. Whatever its application, our distinctive New Abbey Limestone is available only in polished and honed finishes, and it should only be mortar set using white Portland cement or other non-staining mastics.
