Mercy Hospital

Project Principals

Architecture: Cannon Design

Built in the late 1800's this Greek Revival mansion is perched atop a steep seaside bluff with sweeping territorial views of the bay and beyond. This masterful update retains the homes original style and character through the seamless use of antique and newly crafted materials.

The juxtaposition of new and antique is carried into the estate terraces through the use of antique and newly quarried stone from Rhodes Architectural Stone.

Descend the split central staircase of antique granite to the first garden vignette furnished with a fountain of Antique Yangtze with a coping of Cosmos Limestone.

Beyond the tranquil rose garden is a pergola accented with 8-foot carved sandstone columns. Further still, another staircase leads to a secluded outdoor spa and quiet 'zipper' path of more antique granite with curved New Yangtze Limestone herringbone retaining wall.
